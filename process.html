Our process

A defensible investigation begins with a disciplined process.

Structured enough for rigor. Flexible enough for the matter.

A proven framework

Seven steps from concern to clear findings.

The precise scope and sequence vary, but every engagement is grounded in fairness, evidence preservation, transparent methodology, and proportionate inquiry.

01

Initial consultation

Understand the concern and the decision ahead.

We begin with a confidential discussion of the allegations, desired outcomes, stakeholders, legal considerations, timing, and potential conflicts.

  • Initial matter assessment
  • Conflict review
  • Engagement fit and timing
02

Scope & plan

Define the questions the investigation must answer.

We establish the allegations, authority, standard of proof, communication protocols, evidence strategy, witness sequence, and reporting expectations.

  • Written scope
  • Investigation plan
  • Interview and evidence strategy
03

Evidence preservation

Protect the record before facts are lost.

Relevant documents, email, chat, policies, HR records, system data, and other evidence are identified and preserved whenever possible.

04

Interviews

Give each participant a fair and meaningful opportunity to be heard.

Interviews are structured, respectful, thorough, and designed to test accounts against the available evidence.

05

Analysis

Evaluate credibility, consistency, corroboration, and context.

We assess the totality of the evidence, distinguish direct knowledge from assumption, and resolve meaningful conflicts where the evidence permits.

06

Findings report

Explain what was investigated, what was found, and why.

Reports document the allegations, methodology, evidence reviewed, interviews conducted, factual findings, supporting analysis, and recommendations when requested.

07

Follow through

Support the organization after findings are delivered.

We remain available to brief counsel or leadership, answer questions, clarify the record, and assist with implementation planning.

What clients can expect

Professionalism at every stage.

Clear Communication

Defined points of contact, practical updates, and early discussion of material scope changes.

Proportionate Inquiry

A process calibrated to the seriousness, complexity, and evidentiary needs of the matter.

Useful Reporting

Findings written for real decisions—not academic abstraction or unnecessary volume.

Confidential consultation

When the facts matter, begin with a discreet conversation.
