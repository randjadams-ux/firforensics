Services

Investigative support tailored to the allegation—not a template.

Focused engagements for workplace, ethics, executive, and compliance matters.

What we do

Independent investigations that help leaders act with confidence.

Every engagement is scoped around the allegations, available evidence, decision-making needs, and the appropriate level of independence.

01

Workplace investigations

Misconduct, harassment, discrimination, and retaliation.

Objective fact-finding when allegations affect trust, safety, employment decisions, or organizational credibility.

We clarify the allegations, identify relevant records and witnesses, conduct neutral interviews, assess credibility, and document findings under the applicable standard.

Common matters

  • Harassment and discrimination
  • Retaliation and whistleblower concerns
  • Bullying and abusive conduct
  • Policy and code-of-conduct violations
  • Conflicts of interest
02

Executive & sensitive matters

Heightened independence for high-consequence allegations.

Investigations involving senior leaders, board members, public officials, or trusted insiders require exceptional discretion and credibility.

We structure these matters to protect independence, reduce avoidable conflicts, and create a reliable record for counsel, boards, and decision-makers.

Engagement considerations

  • Board or counsel oversight
  • Limited communication protocols
  • Senior-level interviewing
  • Reputational and governance risk
  • Executive-ready reporting
03

Ethics & compliance

Fact-finding where conduct intersects with governance and risk.

Independent review of suspected ethical breaches, reporting integrity concerns, misuse of resources, or compliance-related misconduct.

These matters may involve complex records, competing business explanations, and the need to understand how policies, controls, and leadership expectations operated in practice.

Common matters

  • Ethics hotline allegations
  • Reporting or metrics integrity
  • Misuse of confidential information
  • Compliance control concerns
  • Management override or pressure
04

Interviewing & fact-finding

Focused support when a full investigation is not required.

Targeted interviews, documentary review, chronology development, or discrete fact-finding can strengthen a broader legal or organizational response.

Fir Forensics can support outside counsel, internal legal teams, HR, or compliance leaders with defined investigative workstreams.

Available support

  • Witness and subject interviews
  • Evidence review and chronology
  • Investigative planning
  • Credibility and corroboration analysis
  • Prior-investigation review
05

Title IX & educational institutions

Campus investigations that meet Title IX's procedural requirements.

Colleges, universities, and K-12 institutions face strict procedural obligations when responding to sexual harassment, discrimination, and misconduct complaints under Title IX.

We conduct Title IX-compliant investigations and campus policy reviews with the documentation rigor these matters demand, working alongside Title IX coordinators, general counsel, and administrators.

Common matters

  • Title IX sexual harassment complaints
  • Student and faculty misconduct
  • Campus policy and grievance procedure review
  • Coordinator and administrator support
  • Hearing and appeal process guidance
06

Faith-based & nonprofit organizations

Investigations conducted with the discretion these communities require.

Churches, ministries, and nonprofit boards face unique sensitivities when allegations touch congregational trust, mission, and governance.

We bring the same investigative rigor to these matters while remaining attentive to the pastoral, reputational, and governance considerations that set faith-based and nonprofit engagements apart.

Common matters

  • Clergy and staff misconduct
  • Board and governance concerns
  • Congregational and donor trust issues
  • Policy and safeguarding reviews
  • Confidential reporting support
